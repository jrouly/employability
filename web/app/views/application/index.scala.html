Employability is a research project exploring how universities are preparing students for the workforce. Its name comes from the notion of how employable a student is upon graduation.

How to use

This site is designed to report the results of the project. Use the navigation bar on the left to jump around sections of the website, exploring the inferred topics and related documents. The Topics portal allows you to browse all of the inferred topics. Similarly, the Documents portal allows you to browse a random selection of the modeled documents. Elements in each portal are hyperlinked to one another.

So, if you click on a topic, you will be brought to that topic's page, along with a selection of documents that express that topic at a high relevance.

Similarly, if you click on a document, you will be brought to that document's page, along with all the topics it expresses and their relevance values.

What is a topic?

In the context of this project, a topic is a statistical distribution over a vocabulary. Take the following topic, for example.

@components.topic(mockTopic, link = false)

In this contrived topic, the most frequent terms are "cat" and "dog", with "pig" occurring at a lower frequency and irrelevant words like "robot" occurring almost never. Since this topic would have been automatically inferred in an unsupervised fashion, there's no label for it. But, using human intuition, one might expect this topic to be related to documents about family pets.

What is a document?

A document is a collection of words written (presumably) by a human. Those words contain themes that the author intended to convey in their writing. These themes can be modeled as term frequency distributions (topics).

In the context of this project, a document is either a Job Description or a Course Description. The contents of the document are modeled according to inferred topics.
